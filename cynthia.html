Shall we exercise our brains on lockdown?

Here you can check three ideas of things for you to do in a pandemic to keep yourself creative, learning and productive!

Learn to play an instrument

Learning to play a musical instrument has so many benefits – whether it’s building your confidence, enhancing your memory or widening your social circle. Some use it as a pastime, others do this out of passion for music, and for some it’s a kind of stress buster or meditation. Millions of people all over the world play all kinds of instruments as a way to express themselves. Others simply do it for entertainment, fun or to earn money. But out of so many, which ones are the most popular instruments that are easy to play? The list below shows the most popular and easiest out of all of them.

Keyboard

Keyboards, the electronic piano, are extremely versatile and very low maintenance. Electronic keyboards are capable of recreating a wide range of instrument sounds and synthesizer tones with less complex sound synthesis.

Piano

The piano was first invented around 1700 and is used for many types of music including classical, jazz, traditional and popular. It is highly versatile, has a wide-range and a grand ability to change dynamics.

Electric Guitar

Learning to play the guitar is great for confidence building and developing social skills. Learning to play the guitar enhances fine motor skills in children as well as improving their concentration, memory and focus skills.

Learn a new language

Using your spare time to learn a new language is a great idea! There are plenty of online courses that you can take, or why not hire a private native teacher and have a one-to-one experience to immerse yourself in this new world? Here are some of the options of online courses you could take:

Babbel

Babbel currently has 14 languages and it has access to a learner’s community that can help make your adventure a little more social.

LingQ

LingQ rewards you for helping your peers and also has a massive database of lessons which are composed of text documents accompanied by audio.

FluentU

FluentU takes real-world videos like music videos, movie trailers, news and inspiring talks, and turns them into learning lessons.
