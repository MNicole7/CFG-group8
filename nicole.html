Beat the lockdown blues!

My tips on how to stay healthy during quarantine!

It's easy to neglect your exercise routine, coffee break catch-ups and hobbies during lockdown. Here you'll find my tips on alternative activities to do to keep you mentally and physically healthy.

#1 Get Active

Its no secret that exercise is great for you! It can boost your body-image, mood and is a great stress reliever. However, with gyms closed you may be at a loss for what to do keep active. Here are what me and my family have been up to:

  • Walking: There are thousands of miles of footpaths, bridleways and cycle paths across the UK which you can jog, walk, canter or cycle on. Click here to find which footpath is on your doorstep and go and get some fresh air!
  • Sports: If your lucky enough to be isolating with others, get outside and play some sports. This could be anything: rounders, badminton, football or tennis. You can easily pick up a ball, bats or rackets online or in supermarkets.
  • Home Workouts: If you are missing you gym sessions or having a personal trainer, you can find lots of at home workouts online which are apartment friendly and equipment free! Head over to youtube now and find your new fitness gang!

#2 Keep Rested

Everyone needs a break and its important whilst on quarantine that between childcare, working, studying and housework that you make time for self-care. Here are a few things that I have been doing to unwind after work:

Reading

Reading is known to reduce stress and with millions of books on a range of topics so your sure to find one which suits you. Also, there are many e-books which are free or just 99p so reading doesn't have to break the bank! If you are stuck for what to read check out GoodReads a online community where you can chat with fellow readers, find book reviews and track your book count!

Puzzles

Puzzles can help improve your memory, reduce heart rate, regulate your breathing and even raise your IQ! With charity shops closed, it's hard to find a good puzzle, however there are lots of free virtual puzzles online. The free app Jigsaw Puzzle is available on Apple and has over 20,000 puzzles for you to complete ranging from 400 to 1000+ pieces!

Meditation

There are thousands of mediation videos, tutorials and apps out there. With meditation linked to less anxiety, decreased memory-loss and better sleep quality you should give it a go! #3 Stay Connected

Everyone needs someone to speak too. However with social distancing in place meaning you can't physically meet family and friends as easily you may find it difficult to keep up your ties. Here are a few of my recommendations for staying in contact with others:

  • Quizzes: It's easy to hold a quiz virtually using Microsoft Teams, Skype or Gmail calls. So get your questions prepared, sort out a forfeit for the losers and arrange a get-together online!
  • Social Media: There are lots of social media platforms out there: Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, TikTok to name a few. Share what you have been getting up to in lockdown!
  • Charities: Finally, if you need someone to talk too. There are many great charities out there who are just a call away. A great one for those struggling with mental health is mind click the button below read more:
