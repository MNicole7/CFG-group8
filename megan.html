Life in the NHS as a medical student

I 'm a graduate entry medical student in Swansea and currently working in the NHS during the COVID-19 crisis.
Day to day there have been some new things to get used to, such as trying to social distance whilst in confined spaces and wearing masks all day. The masks vary depending on what you 're doing but we all have to wear basic surgical masks at the very least, unfortunately these have been mass produced very quickly so they irritate your skin terribly! When we have to see suspected COVID patients it requires full PPE which is absolutely sweltering as the gowns we have to wear are made from plastic. I expect the future of the NHS to be quite different with less time spent in hospital if it can be avoided, more virtual clinics, and a more remote way of teaching.

Exercising, back to basics

Since lockdown began I've had to switch up my exercise routine and have started running and doing regular yoga. The running has been really good for my mental health and getting outside in the fresh air is always good! I'm challenging myself to run a bit further and have completed the couch to 5k programme and now I am working on a 5k to 10k programme which is really rewarding. I also downloaded a yoga app which the developers kindly made free for all students and teachers until July, but it was so good that I ended up paying to subscribe to it for a year!

Supporting local businesses (aka eating takeaway)

Supporting local businesses: There are some great restaurants near me that have started to offer takeaway, so once a week my boyfriend and I have been making sure to order from them to help them stay open. One of my favourites is The Pizza Boyz based in Swansea who do delicious homemade style pizzas with original toppings.

They are so good!

I've also been reflecting on my usual shopping habits and trying to lean into a more sustainable, small business focused approach.
